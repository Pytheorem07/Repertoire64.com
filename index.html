How to Use Repertoire64

1. Build Your Openings

Click and drag pieces (or simply click the destination square) to record moves. Under the Build tab, write variation names and comments for any move. Add as many branching alternative moves as you want! Step backward through your moves and the full line stays visible — play a different move and the old continuation is preserved in parentheses, fully clickable, so nothing you've built is ever lost.

2. ChessDB & Lichess Auto-Build Features

Harness the power of the public databases (with fallback option to Lichess Personal Access Tokens) to automatically build opening trees directly on your active variations! You can set custom plies deep, and configure the crawler's max branching limit safely with customized warnings for larger generation counts.

3. Deep Engine & DB Analysis

Stockfish calculates top-tier move options in Standard Algebraic Notation (e.g. e4 or Nf3) alongside evaluation metrics. Click any engine path to automatically execute that sequence on the board. ChessDB query logs keep you aligned with optimal master theory.

4. Spaced repetition & Repertoire Tour

Launch customized study lines in the Train tab. By default, the program auto-plays opponent actions. Use the Watch Rep button below the chessboard to automatically sit back and watch your whole repertoire played out beautifully branch-by-branch!

5. Deviation & Opening Stats Analysis

Under the Games tab, scan recent player archives. Pinpoint exact spots you drifted from prep, or click Opening Stats to parse up to 4000 games instantly, extracting white/black response frequencies and a full analytical index of 75 major chess openings.

6. Accounts & Cloud Sync

Sign in with Google or email (top of the screen) to save your repertoires, Lichess token, and settings to the cloud — sign in anywhere and they're all there. Guest mode is also available but doesn't sync or unlock community features.

7. Community Repertoires

The Community tab lets signed-in users publish repertoires publicly, tag them by opening, and browse others' work sorted by most liked, most viewed, recently popular, or admin picks. Like and comment on repertoires you find useful, or apply to become a Repertoire Specialist — an admin-approved badge that highlights your published work.

8. Username & Messages

Every account gets a username, changeable once for free from the account menu — further changes are sent to an admin for approval. Check the Messages tab for admin notices, warnings, or system-wide announcements; opening it marks everything read.

9. Feedback

Found a bug or have a suggestion? The Feedback tab inside Community sends it straight to the admins for review — no account required.
